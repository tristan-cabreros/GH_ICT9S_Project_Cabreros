Learn Fire Safety
Fire safety refers to the precautions and measures taken to prevent fires and minimize the damage caused by them. It involves a range of practices, such as ensuring the proper installation and maintenance of smoke alarms, identifying and removing potential fire hazards, creating emergency evacuation plans, and knowing how to properly use fire extinguishers. Fire safety is important not only for the protection of property but also for the safety of people. It is a critical aspect of disaster preparedness, and everyone can play a role in ensuring that their home, workplace, and community are safe from fires.

4 Fire Prevention Tips
Be cautious when cooking

Unattended cooking is the leading cause of home fires. When cooking, stay in the kitchen and keep flammable materials like dish towels or curtains away from the stove.
Regularly maintain electrical appliances

Faulty electrical appliances can start fires. To prevent this, make sure to regularly inspect and maintain electrical appliances, such as heaters or air conditioners, and replace any frayed cords or damaged appliances.
Don't smoke indoors

Smoking is a major cause of fires, and smoking indoors can increase the risk. To prevent fires, always smoke outside and properly dispose of cigarette butts in a fire-safe container.
Properly store flammable materials

Flammable materials like gasoline or propane should be stored in a safe location away from heat sources and open flames. Never store these materials in a living area or near electrical outlets.

Things to Remember during a Fire
Stay calm and act quickly

Panic can lead to confusion and poor decision-making during a fire. It's important to remain calm and act quickly to ensure your safety.
Check doors for heat

Before opening any doors, feel the door handle and the top of the door. If it's hot, do not open the door. Instead, try to find another way out.
Exit the building as quickly and safely as possible

If there are other people in the building, alert them to the fire and the need to evacuate. Use fire alarms or call out loudly to ensure everyone is aware of the danger.
Call for help

Once you are out of the building, call for help immediately. Dial 911 or the local emergency number to report the fire and provide your location.
